Colliding

Oklahoma City, OK

Colliding - Maybe

Videos

What began as a college capstone project has become one of Oklahoma City’s most compelling young bands. In 2023, manager Abby rounded up a handful of close friends who played instruments with a simple goal: form a band and book one show before the semester ended. The lineup of Ember Hawkins on vocals, Riley Luttrell and Opie Clonts on guitars, Emerson Hacker on bass, and Zane Cowan on drums is still rolling purely for the fun of hanging with friends and making noise together.

Blending 80s dream pop with 90s alternative rock and shoegaze, their sound sits comfortably alongside Tamaryn, My Bloody Valentine, and Slowdive. Ember's writing draws inspiration from personal relationships—romantic, familial, and self-reflective—crafting songs that explore regret, longing, and the courage it takes to connect.

The latest EP shines brightest on “Maybe,” a slow-building standout about wanting someone but being too scared to say it, hoping they’ll just figure it out—anchored by some of the band’s most carefully crafted harmonies. Already seasoned by Norman Music Festival appearances, spins on The Spy, and coverage in Free Press OKC, Colliding heads into 2026 with a headlining slot at Plaza Festival and plans to book their first out-of-state shows.
