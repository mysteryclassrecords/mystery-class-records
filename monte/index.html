Monte is a five-piece post-rock, art rock, and progressive pop band based in Oklahoma City. The project began in fall 2019 in Norman, OK when Noah Petrie, Santana Spangler-Day, and Sam Stormer first jammed together, quickly recruiting drummer Michael Birney after a handful of early shows as Late Night Flex. After a COVID hiatus and the addition of multi-instrumentalist Rachel Bandy, the group solidified as the close-knit unit that has stayed friends and collaborators for seven years.

Noah Petrie (vox, guitar, bass), Rachel Bandy (bass, trumpet, keys, vox, accordion), Santana Spangler-Day (violin, bass), Michael Birney (drums), and Sam Stormer (guitar) draw from nature, animals, video games, and personal growth to create music full of alternate tunings, odd time signatures, non-traditional structures, and wildly diverse instrumentation. Fans of Black Country, New Road, Black Midi, Jim O’Rourke, and Twine will feel right at home in their evolving sound.

Their third full-length, Fledgling (2023–2025), finally cements that sound—written and recorded with friend and producer Chris Raun, mastered by Timeless Mastering (Alex G, Slowdive), with art by Rachel and lyrics by Noah. After years of packing OKC venues like Speakeasy and Opolis, festival stages across the state, and even Lost N’ Sound in Fort Worth, Monte is ready to release Fledgling, push the music further, and start writing the next chapter.
